Redefining "The Poison" in the AI Era

Traditional toxicology relies on linear models that fail to capture the complexity of biological systems. Deep Homeostatic Modeling uses AI to define the "shape" of health, detecting toxicity not as pathology, but as systemic entropy.

01. The Manifold Hypothesis

Traditional toxicology assumes biological response (y) scales linearly with dose (d): y = f(d) + ε.

In contrast, Deep Homeostatic Modeling posits that healthy biological states lie on a low-dimensional, non-linear manifold (\mathcal{M}) embedded within high-dimensional space.

Key Concept:

Health is not a single point, but a "safe zone" of physiological stability. Toxicity is the vector distance pushing the system off this manifold.

02. The Deep Dysregulation Score (DDS)

We use a Nested Autoencoder to learn the manifold. The model compresses biological data (x) into a latent state (z) and attempts to reconstruct it (\hat{x}).

The toxicity metric is the Reconstruction Error:

DDS_i = ||x_i - D_phi(E_theta(x_i))||^2
  • Low DDS: Data fits the healthy pattern (Homeostasis).
  • High DDS: Data violates healthy correlations (Dysregulation).

03. The Deep Benchmark Dose (dBMD)

We introduce a new regulatory metric. Unlike traditional BMD which looks for a % change in a single mean, dBMD looks for a shift in systemic stability.

Definition:

The dose where the predicted DDS exceeds the baseline mean by 1 standard deviation.

class DeepHomeostaticAutoencoder(nn.Module): def __init__(self, input_dim, latent_dim=10): super().__init__() # --- Encoder: Compresses biology to "Health State" --- self.encoder = nn.Sequential( nn.Linear(input_dim, 64), nn.ReLU(), nn.Linear(64, 32), nn.ReLU(), nn.Linear(32, latent_dim) # Latent Manifold ) # --- Decoder: Reconstructs phenotype --- self.decoder = nn.Sequential( nn.Linear(latent_dim, 32), nn.ReLU(), nn.Linear(32, 64), nn.Linear(64, input_dim) ) def get_dysregulation_score(self, x): """Calculates Manifold Distance (DDS)""" x_hat = self.decoder(self.encoder(x)) dds = torch.mean((x - x_hat) ** 2, dim=1) return dds
